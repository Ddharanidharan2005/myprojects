**Mannargudi** is a town located in the Thiruvarur district of Tamil Nadu, India. Known for its rich cultural heritage and historical significance, Mannargudi is home to the prominent.

**Rajagopalaswamy temple**, a renowned Vaishnavite shrine.

The town was founded during the 7th century by the Medieval Cholas and has since been ruled by various dynasties, including the Chola king Rajadhiraja Chola, the Vijayanagar Empire, and the British Empire.

Mannargudi is also known for its agricultural, metalworking, and weaving industries. The town is well-connected by roadways and railways, with the nearest seaport being Nagapattinam Port and the closest airport being Tiruchirappalli International Airport.
